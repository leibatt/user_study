Overview

In Task 1, you will identify regions around the world with high amounts of snow cover.

What You Need to Know

About the Data Set: The Task 1 data set is a 2D array containing Normalized Difference Snow Index (NDSI) calculations from the MODIS satellite imagery data set. The two dimensions are latitude (x) and longitude (y). A high NDSI value (1.0) means that particular latitude/longitude area contains a high amount of snow. A low value (-1.0) means that particular part of the earth does not contain snow. Regions that consist mainly of water (i.e. oceans, lakes, rivers) are ignored, and appear white in the visualizations.

Goal(s): Find visualizations where half the visualization consists of high NDSI (> 0.5) values (i.e. regions of the earth with 50% snow cover).
